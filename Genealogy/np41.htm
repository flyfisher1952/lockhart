BIOGRAPHY: He died of a heart attack. He had come in for lunch and left his

his widow, Bertha, and then for Chet. Chet died in 1996 and had decided to
sell it, but couldn't do it for sentimental reasons. So, Erma, his wife sold
it in 1998. The new owner completely cleared it off to put more ground into
production. So th eonly thing left on it is the Cemetary on the East side.

Note for:   Alexander Rankin,   1662 -          Index

Individual Note:
     BIOGRAPHY: During the persecution in Scotland in the 16th century, Alexander
Rankin lived there with his three sons. Two of his sons were martyred for
their religious beliefs. One being killed on the highway and another
suffocating in a smokehouse while trying to elude his persuers. Alexander and
his son William fled to Derry county, Ireland in 1688 where they were
participants in the siege of Londondery in 1689. Alexander's name is signed to
the Petition of Thanks to Almighty God and to William, King of Orange for his
timely assistance in raising the siege in August, 1689.
