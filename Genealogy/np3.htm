Note for:   John Niles,   1603 - 8 Feb 1693         Index

Individual Note:
     BIOGRAPHY: By Joel Nelson Eno A.M. of Brooklyn, N.Y.

BIOGRAPHY: John Niles, of Dorchester and Braintree, Massachusettes, ancestor
of all of this surname in the northern part of the U.S. was born, probably in
England 1603 and died in Braintree February 8, 1693/4, the entry of his death
in the town record calling him "Old John Niles", giving his age about 91. (He
removed to Braintree 1638 and was a weaver). He married first probably 1636
Jane Reed of Braintree, who died may 15, 1654, and secondly Hannan*----Ames,
widow of Wm Ames, who died Jan 31, 1703.

BIOGRAPHY: John Niles was in Dorchester in Sept 1634 and settled in Braintree
in 1636.

BIOGRAPHY: (Please forgive the poor grammar, these are Joel's words.)

Note for:   Charles Wilcox Rankin,   27 Oct 1909 - 14 Apr 1989         Index

Individual Note:
     This is my maternal grandfather, although I never met him. I was raised
thinking that Chester Hattervig was my grandad, and only found out that he was
my grandmother's 2nd husband after I joined the Church and started doing
genealogy.

My mother got in touch with her aunt (Francis Alley) and grandmother (Flora
Mae) when we moved to California. We used to visit them in Chula Vista near
San Diego. I later learned that we had once come to visit when he was mowing
his lawn. He lived across the street from Aunt Francis who cared for her
mother (Flora) in their home. When we were getting out of the car, mom walked
over to talk to him (Wilcox, her dad, our grandad). He turned and walked into
his house, slamming the door. He then forbid his son Charles from visiting
his Aunt (Francis) while we were there.

This infuriated Francis' husband who we called Uncle Al even though his name
was Corwin Clarence (he went by CC or Al) who forbid Wilcox from entering his
house, effectively barring him from visiting his mother. Aunt Francis still
has a rather low opinion of him. I talked to her the other day (26 Dec 1996)
and when I asked about him, she said, "You didn't miss much."

Note for:   Soren Derman,    -          Index

Individual Note:
     Changed name to Jorgensen when he entered the United States. Was a very tough
but fair man. Red hair. He was very wealthy and he lost all of his money in
the crash of the 1920s. He gave a Farm with a house, horses, pigs, &
machinery to all of his children when they got married except for Ervin & Erma.
     they were married after the crash of the 20s and so he had no money left.
When he died most of the children owed them money and his wife Kristina let
them out of their debts.

I have talked to Grama (Erma) and she now says the spelling of the surname is
"Derman" and that we may have some relatives near Warren, Pennsylvania. These
people would be related via a brother or uncle (grama wasn't clear, I think it
was Soren's brother but I'm not sure) of her or her dad Soren.

Grama said that Soren was about 15-16 when he came to the US.
